My research spans the intersection of **Artificial Intelligence**, **Applied Machine Learning**, and **Decision Analytics**, with applications in energy systems and smart infrastructure. I focus on building AI/ML solutions that are not only accurate but transparent, explainable, and deployable in real-world settings.

## Research Interests

- **Foundation Models & LLMs** — zero-shot semantic mapping, ontology-constrained models, AI-assisted document processing (e.g., NEPA permitting, building metadata normalization)
- **Explainable AI (XAI)** — interpretable ML for building energy benchmarking, retrofit decisions, and infrastructure planning
- **Spatiotemporal Learning** — time-series prediction for dynamical systems, inference workload characterization, multivariate forecasting for facility operations
- **Multi-Objective Decision Analytics** — AI-enabled visual analytics for large-scale energy infrastructure planning (pyMOODS), probabilistic modeling for policy impact
- **Energy Systems & Smart Infrastructure** — building controls, demand flexibility, HVAC optimization, electricity market analytics, retrofit planning
- **Optimization under Uncertainty** — stochastic decision frameworks, model predictive control, reinforcement learning for congestion management

## Projects

**Operationalizing AI for Enhanced Permitting Processes** (US DOE Grid Deployment Office, 2026)  
Developing AI tools to streamline NEPA permitting workflows for transmission infrastructure, including the PermitCE tool for categorical exclusion reporting.

**LLM Model Pipeline for Building Semantic Modeling** (US DoD Washington HQ Services, 2026)  
Designing LLM-based pipelines to automate semantic tagging and normalization of building metadata to standard ontologies (Brick Schema).

**pyMOODS: Multi-Objective Decision Support for Energy Infrastructure Planning** (PNNL ECOMP Initiative, 2023–2025)  
AI-enabled visual analytics framework for multi-criteria decision-making in large-scale electricity infrastructure planning.

**Integrated HPC-Enabled Co-Design and Multi-Criteria Decision-Making Framework** (PNNL ECOMP Initiative, 2023–2025)  
HPC-integrated framework for scalable co-design and optimization across energy system planning scenarios.

**IMEMP: Integrated Methane Emission Monitoring Platform** (US DOE FECM, 2024–2025)  
AI-assisted platform for monitoring and analyzing methane emissions in collaboration with industry partners NOV and Acacia.
